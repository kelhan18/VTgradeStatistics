About me: Keller Han

I'm a rising Junior studying Computer Science at Virginia Tech. I learned Python and gained a solid foundation for it as I used it as my main programming language for my summer 2017 internship with CACI. With the language's simplistic style and powerful data-science libraries, I decided to put it to practice and analyze some data. VT Grade Distribution data was something that's been in my sights for a while, and I decided to look at that data and organize it in a way that would benefit me during course selection. I decided to take the most recent CS Grade data, since Fall 2014, and organize it in a easy-to-read table.

My Website: The Purpose

This website was created for personal use at first, then released to friends at their request. In a world where grades are a dominant factor in school, every student should want to see past statistics on professors and develop a better understanding of which teachers to request. VT Grade Distribution is a tool released by VT that allows students to see data from a specific semester. This website combines data from the previous six semesters to create a more clear perception on professors as a whole. Since this website was created for personal use and for friends, not every major is listed.
